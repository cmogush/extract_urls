Origins of Irish-American Culture









Between 1845 and 1852, a terrible famine swept over the country of Ireland. It was a period of mass starvation and disease during which many people died. At least a million more people fled the country in search of a better life. For many of them, that meant coming to America.

Walt Whitman is widely considered to be one of America’s greatest poets. In his poem “Old Ireland,” Whitman memorializes the great Irish famine, casting Ireland in the role of a mother grieving over the loss of her children.
