Origins of African-American Culture





 



Dramatic monologue is a words-based performance that offers great insight into the feelings of the speaker. African Americans have been responsible for creating many of the art forms thought to be uniquely American. In the video podcast, African-American actor, director, and educator Harvey Johnson, gives a dramatic monologue of “O Black and Angry Bards,” which was written by the celebrated writer, political leader, and early civil rights activist, James Weldon Johnson.
